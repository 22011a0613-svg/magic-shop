Our mission

Crafts that survive should also thrive

Every handwoven saree is a sentence in a story that's been told for centuries. We exist to make sure that story never ends.

🧵

Sustain artisans

We pay artisans directly and fairly, ensuring their craft becomes a livelihood their children will want to continue — not abandon.

📖

Spread the story

Each product comes with the craft's history, the artisan's face, and the technique behind it — education built into every purchase.

🌍

Connect worlds

From a weaver's home in Pochampally to a living room in London — we build the bridge that makes this exchange possible and meaningful.

The hands behind the magic

Meet the artisans

These aren't just suppliers. They are guardians of India's living heritage — and the heart of everything we do.

🧑‍🎨
Pochampally Ikat
Rajamma Goud
Pochampally, Telangana
Weaving ikat since the age of 12, Rajamma creates sarees whose patterns take three days just to plan. Her family has woven for four generations.
🎨
Nirmal Paintings
Venkat Rao
Nirmal, Telangana
Each of Venkat's paintings begins with a cow-dung base coat — a 500-year-old tradition. He is one of fewer than 40 master painters still practicing.
🏺
Bidriware
Mohammed Iqbal
Bidar, Karnataka
Mohammed inlays silver into blackened zinc-copper alloy by hand — a technique from the Bahmani Sultanate era that he learned from his grandfather.
